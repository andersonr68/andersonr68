Charkhi Dadri mid-air collision



The Charkhi Mid-air collision occured near the village of Charkhi Dadri in Haryana, India in November 12, 1996. It involve two aircraft that collided and resulted in the death of 349 people that where on board both aircraft.


Reason for Crash

This aviation tragedy unfolded due to a tragic breakdown in communication and coordination and thick fog and poor visibility that made it worse. Saudi Arabuan Airlines Flight 763 a Boeing 747, and Kazakhstan Airlines Flight 1907 and Ilyushin II-76 were both simultaneously cleared for descent by the same trafic controller. The series of miscommunications nad misunderstandings continued which led to both aircraft descending along the same flight path. This resulted in the mid-air collision.

Changes Implemented after

This accident had a significant impact on the aviation industry. It led to important changes in air traffic control procedures and safety potocols. This accident highlighted the critical need for improved communication and coordination between air traffic controllers and flight crews, specially in dangerous weather conditions. Advance collision avoidance systems where implemented to reduce the likelihood of similar accidents.
